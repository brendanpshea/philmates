# Image prompts — Patient Autonomy ("House Calls in the Hedgerow")

The lesson ships with **hand-authored SVGs** (`/assets/*.svg`) so it works with zero
external art. To enhance later with an image generator, generate the asset below,
drop the PNG in `/assets/` with the matching name (e.g. `fox.png`), and swap the
`src` in `index.html`. Aim for the *best* version of each: fun, gamelike, memorable,
and informative — the character should embody the consent issue it stands for.

The cast is a **storybook hedgerow** of animal patients, so full character portraits
are fair game (no real likeness to fail at). Lean into warm Beatrix-Potter charm
rendered in crisp pixels — each animal's personality *is* the teaching point about
when consent is genuine.

## House style (prepend to every prompt)

> **16-bit SNES/JRPG pixel art**, storybook-warm. Hard pixel edges, no
> anti-aliasing, subtle dithered shading, a chunky silhouette that reads clearly at
> 64 px. Tight palette drawn from the PhilMates UI: void background `#11131f`, panel
> slate `#1d2235`, ink `#eef1ff`, and four accent inks — go-green `#46e07a`, magenta
> `#ff6ad5`, info-blue `#4cc2ff`, amber `#ffcf5a`. Soft golden cottage-lamp lighting.
> **No text, no lettering, no watermark, no signature.** Transparent or `#11131f`
> background. Square (1:1); character art is a centered bust.

## Patients & cast

### `doctor-bag.png` — the travelling clinic
> A classic black leather country-doctor's bag with a worn brass clasp and a small
> embossed green cross; a corner of a stethoscope and a rolled bandage peek out;
> resting on a mossy hedgerow stump.
> *Teaches:* the house-call frame — care brought to the patient, on the patient's
> own ground.

### `peter-rabbit.png` — Peter Rabbit
> A brown rabbit in a slightly-too-big sky-blue jacket with brass buttons, tall
> ears, one paw behind his back and a guilty sidelong glance.
> *Teaches:* the patient who nods along but didn't really follow doctor's orders —
> was that informed agreement, or just politeness?

### `jemima-duck.png` — Jemima Puddle-Duck
> A trusting white duck in a blue poke-bonnet and pink-trimmed shawl, soft naive
> eyes turned hopefully upward.
> *Teaches:* how easily a too-trusting patient is misled — the case for actively
> protecting autonomy, not just not overriding it.

### `squirrel.png` — Squirrel Nutkin
> A cheeky russet-red squirrel with bold ear-tufts and an enormous curling bushy
> tail, wide impulsive eyes and a daredevil grin, caught mid-spring.
> *Teaches:* the patient who refuses advice on sheer principle — respecting choices
> you think are reckless.

### `hedgehog.png` — Mrs Tiggy-Winkle
> A stout, sensible brown hedgehog washerwoman in a white frilled mob-cap and apron,
> spines neat as a brush, kindly no-nonsense eyes, a tiny flat-iron in one paw.
> *Teaches:* the steady caregiver — supportive without being paternalistic.

### `fox.png` — the sandy-whiskered gentleman
> A suave orange fox in a tweed jacket with long sandy whiskers, narrowed scheming
> eyes and a too-charming smile, bushy tail curling like a question mark.
> *Teaches:* manipulation dressed as courtesy — the threat that makes "consent"
> hollow.

> Note: characters are stylized, gently grown-up takes on Beatrix Potter figures;
> the bioethics content (informed consent; Emanuel's four models of the
> doctor–patient relationship) is the real lesson.
