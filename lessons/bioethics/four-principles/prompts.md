# Image prompts — Bioethics: The Four Principles ("Rounds with Van Helsing")

The lesson ships with **hand-authored SVGs** (`/assets/*.svg`) so it works with zero
external art. To enhance later with an image generator, generate the asset below,
drop the PNG in `/assets/` with the matching name (e.g. `vampire.png`), and swap the
`src` in `index.html`. Aim for the *best* version of each: fun, gamelike, memorable,
and informative — the picture should carry the story and the principle, not just
label a name.

The cast here is **invented** (a monster ward), so full character portraits are
fair game — there's no real likeness to fail at, and a strong character sells the
case. Lean into mood, lighting, and a clear silhouette.

## House style (prepend to every prompt)

> **16-bit SNES/JRPG pixel art.** Hard pixel edges, no anti-aliasing, subtle
> dithered shading, a chunky silhouette that reads clearly at 64 px. Tight palette
> drawn from the PhilMates UI: void background `#11131f`, panel slate `#1d2235`, ink
> `#eef1ff`, and four accent inks — go-green `#46e07a`, magenta `#ff6ad5`, info-blue
> `#4cc2ff`, amber `#ffcf5a`. Moody single-source lamp/screen glow, like a gothic
> hospital ward at night. **No text, no lettering, no watermark, no signature.**
> Transparent or `#11131f` background. Square (1:1); character art is a centered
> bust.

## Cast

### `van-helsing.png` — Doctor Van Helsing (the narrator)
> A warm, grizzled vampire-hunter turned bedside physician: grey beard, small round
> spectacles catching a glint, a dark-green frock coat over a white collar, a
> stethoscope around his neck — and a wooden stake tucked among the vials of his
> open doctor's bag. Wise, kindly, unflappable, lit by soft lamp-glow.
> *Teaches:* the steady guide who weighs all four principles at each bedside.

### `vampire.png` — the Count (a patient)
> A pale aristocratic vampire in a hospital gown half-hidden under a high-collared
> black cape with crimson lining; slicked widow's-peak hair, glowing red eyes,
> delicate fangs, an IV line of synthetic blood in one arm. Imperious but plainly
> ailing.
> *Teaches:* autonomy vs. his own dangerous appetites — respecting a patient's
> choices even when you fear them.

### `werewolf.png` — the werewolf (a patient)
> A shaggy brown werewolf perched on an exam table, pointed ears, amber eyes, fangs,
> and a comically re-wrapped white head-bandage (he keeps re-injuring himself),
> looking sheepish.
> *Teaches:* beneficence and non-maleficence — helping a patient who keeps doing
> himself harm.

### `creature.png` — the Creature (a vulnerable patient)
> Frankenstein's creature drawn gentle and sorrowful: a flat-topped stitched head,
> neck bolts catching a faint blue spark, mismatched sickly-green skin, soft
> pleading eyes, a blanket around the shoulders.
> *Teaches:* justice — the patient whose standing is easiest to overlook still gets
> equal moral weight.

## Objects

### `scales.png` — justice / fair allocation
> A level, clinical two-pan balance in cool steel-blues against a faint ward-tile
> backdrop, both pans exactly equal — impartial, institutional, calm.
> *Teaches:* distributive justice — who fairly gets the one scarce dose?

### `blood-vial.png` — the scarce synthetic blood
> A stoppered glass vial of luminous deep-crimson synthetic blood with a soft inner
> glow and a single bright highlight, a blank apothecary label, drawn like a rare
> *quest-item resource* in its inventory slot.
> *Teaches:* the one scarce resource the whole dilemma turns on.

> Note: the monstrous characters are stylized educational illustrations; the
> framework they dramatize (Beauchamp & Childress's four principles) is the real
> content.
