# Image prompts — Utilitarianism lesson

The lesson ships with **hand-authored SVGs** (`/assets/*.svg`) so it works with zero
external art. If you want richer, raster (PNG) illustrations later, generate them
with the prompts below and drop the files in `/assets/` with the matching name
(e.g. `bentham.png`), then swap the `src` in `index.html`.

**House style (use on every prompt):**
> 16-bit SNES-era pixel art, limited palette, crisp hard edges, dark slate-blue
> background (#1d2235), centered character bust, no text, no watermark, 1:1 or 4:5.

| File | Subject | Prompt addition |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| `cake.png` | The decadent dessert | A single lavish slice of chocolate cake topped with gold leaf and a cherry, on a fancy plate, faint sparkles — the story's "$40 cake." |
| `scales.png` | Balance scales | A pixel-art balance scale, one pan glowing green (happiness), the other magenta (suffering), slightly tilted toward green. |
| `epicurus.png` | Epicurus | Ancient Greek philosopher bust, olive laurel wreath, white toga, serene smile, a small garden of green plants behind him. |
| `bentham.png` | Jeremy Bentham | 18th-century English gentleman, white wavy hair, brown coat, white cravat, faint confident smile. |
| `mill.png` | John Stuart Mill | Victorian gentleman, high balding forehead, large mutton-chop sideburns, dark suit, blue tie, thoughtful expression. |
| `singer.png` | Modern philosopher | Contemporary man, grey hair, round glasses, casual blue collared shirt, warm friendly expression. |

> Note: these are stylized educational depictions, not photoreal likenesses.
