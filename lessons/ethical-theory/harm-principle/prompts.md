# Image-generation prompts — The Emerald Constitution (Harm Principle)

Hand-authored 16-bit SVGs are in `assets/`. Use these prompts to generate richer
raster (PNG) versions if desired; keep the 16-bit console-era aesthetic consistent
with the other lessons. Swap the `.svg` references in `index.html` to `.png` after
generating.

Shared style suffix for every prompt:
> 16-bit SNES-era pixel art, limited palette, dark indigo background (#1d2235),
> crisp pixel edges, no anti-aliasing, no text, centered subject, 4:5 portrait crop.

## ozma
Princess Ozma of Oz: a young queen in an emerald-green gown with gold trim, dark
hair, a slim gold circlet with two red poppies tucked into it, holding a rolled
antique scroll across her arms, calm and resolute expression.

## old-constitution
A tattered antique constitution scroll on wooden rollers: yellowed parchment with
a torn bottom edge, faded ink clauses, several lines angrily crossed out in red,
two large ink blots, water stains.

## poppy-field
A field of oversized scarlet poppies at dusk, pale soporific fumes drifting off
the blooms, a small traveler asleep among the stems with "zzz" pixels above, a
crooked wooden warning sign at the field's edge.

## bridge
Mill's bridge case: a rickety rope-and-plank footbridge spanning a deep gorge over
a river far below, one plank missing and another snapping mid-fall, a traveler
with a walking stick pausing at the near edge, about to step on.

## emerald-square
A public square in the Emerald City: green crystalline towers with glowing gem
windows in the background, a pamphleteer standing on a wooden soapbox with one arm
raised holding a leaflet, a small crowd of three or four listeners, one holding a
pamphlet.

## quill-signature
Ratification: a fresh clean parchment on a dark wooden desk, a white feather quill
mid-signature with an ink flourish, an ink pot, and a bright green wax seal with
ribbon beside the document.

## mill (relic card — copied from utilitarianism lesson)
Relic card, 80×108 frame with gold accent, dark inner field, rank gem and rarity
pips: an open book lit from above by a rising flame with small light rays —
Mill's emblem (higher pleasures / the light of open discussion).
