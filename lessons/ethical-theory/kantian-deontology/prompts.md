# Image prompts — Kantian Deontology ("The Cat at the Door")

The lesson ships with **hand-authored SVGs** (`/assets/*.svg`) so it works with zero
external art. To enhance later with an image generator, generate the asset below,
drop the PNG in `/assets/` with the matching name (e.g. `kant.png`), and swap the
`src` in `index.html`. Aim for the *best* version of each: fun, gamelike, memorable,
and informative — the picture should teach the idea, not just label a name.

## House style (prepend to every prompt)

> **16-bit SNES/JRPG pixel art.** Hard pixel edges, no anti-aliasing, subtle
> dithered shading, a chunky silhouette that still reads clearly at 64 px. Tight
> palette drawn from the PhilMates UI: void background `#11131f`, panel slate
> `#1d2235`, ink `#eef1ff`, and four accent inks — go-green `#46e07a`, magenta
> `#ff6ad5`, info-blue `#4cc2ff`, amber `#ffcf5a`. Dramatic single-source "screen
> glow" lighting. **No text, no lettering, no numerals, no watermark, no
> signature.** Transparent or `#11131f` background. **Objects/characters** are
> square (1:1); **thinker relic cards** are a tall card (4:5).

## Relic-card frame (use for every philosopher)

We never draw a real thinker's face — at pixel size a likeness just reads as a
generic person, so recognition never lands. Instead each thinker is a **collectible
"relic card"** whose emblem *encodes their core idea* (a mnemonic you can collect).

> Render as a tall pixel **relic card**: a chunky near-black `#050610` outer border,
> a glowing 2 px inner frame in the card's **accent color**, a faceted rank-gem set
> in the top bar, and three rarity-pips along the bottom edge. One emblem floats
> centered on the slate panel, lit from above like a museum artifact; the accent
> color tints the frame, gem, pips, and the emblem's highlights.

## Thinkers

### `kant.png` — Immanuel Kant · accent **blue**
> Emblem: a vast deep-indigo starry night arched over a glowing brass mariner's
> compass; the needle holds unwaveringly to a single bright pole-star, the compass
> face etched with radiating "universal law" lines.
> *Teaches:* "the starry heavens above and the moral law within" — one fixed law,
> the same for every rational agent, pointing the same way for all.

### `korsgaard.png` — Christine Korsgaard · accent **magenta**
> Emblem: an ornate heraldic badge of *standing* on the panel — a glowing paw-print
> at its center crowned by a small radiant heart, with a tiny mouse silhouette
> stepping up onto the shield as a new equal.
> *Teaches:* "fellow creatures" — beings for whom things can go well or badly are
> ends in themselves too, widening Kant's circle of who counts.

### `oneill.png` — Onora O'Neill · accent **green**
> Emblem: a golden key floating before a keyhole shaped like a human silhouette; the
> key's bow is formed of two clasped hands (freely given consent). Off to the sides,
> two faint icons hang broken and locked-out: a masquerade mask (deception) and a
> snapped chain (coercion).
> *Teaches:* the practical test of the Formula of Humanity — could everyone affected
> *actually consent*? Deception and coercion are the paradigm ways to treat someone
> as a mere means.

## Characters & objects

### `cat-at-door.png` — the cat (the "murderer at the door")
> A sly blue-grey cat filling a narrow, dimly lit doorway, backlit into a menacing
> silhouette: two slitted glowing-green eyes and a single gleaming fang-grin, one
> paw curling around the doorframe, a faint magenta danger rim-light.
> *Teaches:* the classic test case — the killer asks where your friend is hiding;
> may you lie?

### `mouse.png` — the mouse (you)
> A small round brown mouse pressed into a corner, oversized worried eyes, big
> paper-thin ears lit from behind, a single sweat-bead, trembling whiskers. Cute and
> sympathetic, a JRPG critter.
> *Teaches:* the innocent the cat is hunting — and, later, the *real* animal whose
> own standing is in question.

### `crown.png` — the Kingdom of Ends
> An ornate golden crown ringed with many equal jewels, each gem softly lit like a
> seated legislator, hovering above a round-table glow.
> *Teaches:* the community of rational agents who each both *make* and *obey* the
> moral law — treat everyone as a co-author of it.

### `gear-machine.png` — the Universalizer
> A clockwork logic-engine of interlocking green and magenta gears, a slot at the
> top feeding in a small blank maxim-card and a result-window below it lit with a
> glowing green ✓ or magenta ✗ (as an *icon*, not text).
> *Teaches:* run your maxim through the machine — could it hold as a universal law
> without contradiction?

> Note: thinker cards are stylized educational emblems, never photoreal likenesses —
> the *ideas* are the content.
